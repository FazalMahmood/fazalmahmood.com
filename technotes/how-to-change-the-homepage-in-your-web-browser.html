How to Change Your Home Page in Different Web Browsers

What is a Home Page?
The home page is the first page your web browser opens when you start it. It often shows your most visited sites or content based on your interests.

Google Chrome:

  1. Open Chrome and click the three vertical dots in the top right corner.
  2. Select "Settings."
  3. Scroll down to the "On startup" section and choose "Open a specific page or set of pages."
  4. Click "Add a new page" to type or paste the URL of your new home page.

Mozilla Firefox:

  1. Click the three horizontal lines in the top right corner and choose "Options."
  2. In the "Home Page" section, enter the URL of your new home page or click "Use Current Pages" to set your open tabs as home pages.

Microsoft Edge:

  1. Open Edge and click the three horizontal dots in the top right corner.
  2. Select "Settings" and then "Open Microsoft Edge with."
  3. Choose "A specific page or pages" and enter the URL of your new home page.

Apple Safari:

  1. Open Safari and click the gear icon in the top right corner.
  2. Choose "Preferences" and then "Homepage."
  3. Select the "Homepage" option and enter the URL of your new home page.

That's it! You've successfully changed your home page in your web browser.
